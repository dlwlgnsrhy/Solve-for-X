# Phase 1.5 | SRE Automation: Building the Omnichannel Pipeline

In our ongoing journey to build a legacy with AI, we have reached an important milestone in Phase 1.5 of our project roadmap. This phase focuses on enhancing the integration between our brand website and backend systems, specifically through SRE (Site Reliability Engineering) automation. The primary objectives of this phase include automating the deployment of our tech blog across multiple channels and dynamically linking modal content to live service resources.

### Tech Blog Omnichannel Automation

One of the key achievements in this phase is the completion of our tech blog's omnichannel automation. Previously, our Git Commit parsing script was limited to publishing content externally. We have now integrated a four-step inference process using our local AI (LM Studio) to transform commit messages into polished English narratives. These narratives are then seamlessly deployed not only on our Next.js website but also on Dev.to, ensuring consistent SEO and visibility across multiple platforms.

### Modal and Live Service Resource Integration

Another significant advancement is the dynamic linking of modal content to live service resources. We have successfully connected Google Play Store links for our Life-Log app and Medium blog posts directly to the main page cards. This enhancement not only improves user experience but also strengthens our basecamp functionality by providing immediate access to essential services.

### Future Directions

Looking ahead, we plan to further enhance our backend systems by developing the 'Legacy_Core' engine in Phase 2. This will involve building a central hub server using Spring Boot, which will manage all app data immutably. Additionally, we are working on client edge applications in Phase 3, specifically the SFX Life-Log app for iOS and Android.

### Conclusion

By automating our tech blog deployment and dynamically linking modal content to live service resources, we have taken a significant step towards building a robust and efficient system. This phase has not only improved our operational efficiency but also laid the foundation for future expansions and integrations.
