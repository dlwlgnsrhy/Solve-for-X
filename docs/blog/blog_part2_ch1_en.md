# [Philosophy Series] Chapter 1. In an Era Where Intelligence is like Air, the Value of 'Originality' Skyrockets
**Subtitle: The Paradox of the Generative AI Era: Finding the Real in the Midst of Inflation**

Throughout the previous [Engineering Series], I shared the journey of building a completely autonomous, "hands-off" development team right on my Mac. It was a rigorous technical saga involving a massive local `Qwen 35B` model, OS-level session controls to eradicate zombie processes, and overcoming asynchronous network deadlocks.

While it was a fascinating technical endeavor, I ended the series with a fundamental question:

> "Why go through all this immense trouble? If you had just hooked up to the ChatGPT or Claude APIs, you could have built this ten times faster and easier."

To answer this question, we must look beyond the code and confront the massive paradigm shift we are currently experiencing: the "Singularity" and the "Commoditization of Intelligence."

### Intelligence Becomes as Common as Air

In the past, 'Intelligence' was a scarce and highly expensive resource. Designing an elegant app architecture, writing seamless code, or drafting a deeply logical essay were tasks reserved for a small fraction of highly trained professionals.

However, the explosive advancement of Large Language Models (LLMs) has completely shattered this equation. Intelligence is now as cheap and abundant as electricity or air. With a few lines of a prompt, an AI can generate perfect boilerplate code, write eloquent essays, and even fix its own bugs in seconds. (The autonomous system I built in Part 1 is living proof of this.)

We have entered an era where anyone can instantly produce above-average output. I call this the "Inflation of Intelligence."

### The Paradox of Abundance: The Value of the Real

Following the basic principles of economics, when supply increases exponentially, value drops. The internet is already flooding with 'synthetic data' generated by AI. AI-written blog posts, AI-drawn illustrations, AI-generated code.

Ironically, as these outputs become increasingly perfect, the 'perfectly generated output' itself loses its ability to inspire. Perfection has simply become the new baseline.

In this ocean of synthetic data, what is the one thing that will not only retain its value but actually skyrocket in worth?

The answer is irreplaceable "Originality."

### The Surge of Originality: Human Time and Mortality

AI can persuasively simulate human empathy and perform complex logical reasoning. However, an AI has never "lived" through time, it has never experienced physical pain, and above all, it does not face "Mortality."

The joy and sorrow that can only be experienced by a finite being, a clumsy but sincere line in a diary, the record of a conversation with a loved one, and the legacy left behind while preparing for the end of life.

These raw experiences and memories are absolute "Originals" that no amount of prompt engineering can synthesize. As intelligence becomes as common as air, the finite nature of humans and the data derived from it—what I call the "Soul's Log"—paradoxically becomes the most scarce and valuable asset in the world.

This is exactly why I anchored my app ecosystem—`Imjong Care`, `Memento Mori`, and `Legacy Vault`—on the philosophy of "Living Well = Dying Well." These projects are not factories for generating synthetic data. They are 'vessels' designed to use technology to capture, preserve, and prove the deepest essence and originality of human beings.

### Who Should Guard This Invaluable Originality?

If you agree that our most intimate records of experiences, memories, life, and death will become the most valuable "Original Data" in the world, a natural question follows.

**"Can we really just hand over this incredibly precious and private original data to the cloud servers of Big Tech corporations?"**

This is the true reason why I rejected convenient commercial cloud APIs and stubbornly built an independent local LLM infrastructure on the Mac in the corner of my room. 

In Chapter 2, I will dive into data sovereignty, existential crises, and **"Why I cannot entrust my soul to Big Tech clouds."**
