# [Philosophy Series] Chapter 2. Why I Cannot Entrust My Soul to Big Tech Clouds
**Subtitle: The Existential Crisis of Rented Servers**

In Chapter 1, we established that in an era where intelligence becomes as common as air, the most scarce and valuable asset will be human 'Originality' and the raw records of our finite lives. This realization immediately begs a chilling question:

Where are we currently storing this invaluable 'Originality'?
For the vast majority of us, the answer is the massive data centers of Big Tech corporations—iCloud, Google Drive, AWS.

### The Illusion of Ownership: We Are Just Renters

Modern individuals believe that the photos saved on their phones, the intimate diaries in their note apps, and their health data are entirely their own property. However, this is a massive illusion.

We are merely paying a few dollars a month to rent space within an invisible iron cage built by Big Tech. We do not hold the power cord to the servers. The moment corporate policies change or a service is sunset, decades of our digital selves can evaporate into thin air.

### The Invisible Censor: Algorithmic Filtering

An even more terrifying issue is censorship.
Our deepest emotions, our sometimes raw and unconventional thoughts, and our unfiltered contemplations on life and death (like the core concept of `Memento Mori`) are perfect 'Originals' in their own right.

However, the moment this original data is uploaded to a global platform's server, it falls under the surveillance of ruthless AI moderators. What if my honest reflections on death are flagged by an automated mechanical standard as a "violation of community guidelines"? The records containing my soul would be classified as 'inappropriate content' and deleted without any opportunity for appeal.

Should we really allow our digital legacy to be tailored and filtered by the Terms of Service of Silicon Valley corporations?

### Account Suspension: Digital Death by a Single Click

Imagine waking up one morning to find that your Google or Apple account has been hacked, or permanently suspended for an unknown reason.

Your emails, the photos stored in the cloud, the ideas jotted down in your notes, and even the contacts of your loved ones—gone. In the cloud era, an account suspension is not just an inconvenience; it represents a "Digital Death." It is an existential crisis where decades of proof that you existed in the world are completely wiped out by a single corporate click.

### Searching for a Safe Ark

If my original data is truly going to be my legacy, its survival must not be subjected to a corporation's profit model or shifting terms of service.

I wanted the convenience of a massive AI model writing code and analyzing data for me, but the absolute control and the repository of that data had to remain strictly within me and my devices. A closed, independent ecosystem that can survive eternally even if disconnected from the outside world. That is the exact reason why I struggled so hard to build my infrastructure entirely in a local environment.

Breaking free from the control of giant clouds, cultivating an intelligence that thinks solely for me, strictly within my device. 
In Chapter 3, we will delve deeply into the final defense line designed to shatter this existential crisis: the philosophy of **On-device AI and the 'Origin' project**.
